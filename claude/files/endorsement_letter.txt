The manuscript, titled "Topological Origin of Quantum Bell
Correlations: From 720° Spinor Knots to SU(2) Noncommutativity",
presents a derivation of the Tsirelson bound S = 2√2 from classical
field topology. The argument proceeds in five steps:

  1. A background-free nonlinear field equation, derived from three
     postulates, supports topological solitons with internal
     directional structure.

  2. The solitons have rotational zero modes, parametrized by an
     SU(2) collective coordinate.

  3. Quantization of the collective coordinate produces a Hamiltonian
     whose eigenstates are SU(2) irreducible representations - this
     is the standard Skyrme-Adkins-Nappi-Witten construction.

  4. The measurement operators are SU(2) generators, which
     necessarily fail to commute.

  5. The Finkelstein-Rubinstein constraint forces solitons of odd
     topological charge to be 720° spinors; the noncommutative
     SU(2) algebra acting on the singlet state yields CHSH S = 2√2.

The originality of the work lies not in inventing new mechanisms,
but in the synthesis: the same machinery that gives Skyrmions
their spin-1/2 also explains the Bell inequality bound.

The paper makes a testable prediction distinguishing it from
standard quantum mechanics: a nonzero cross-pair correlation
between two independent entangled photon pairs sharing the same
physical environment.
